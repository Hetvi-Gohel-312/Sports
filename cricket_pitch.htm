15 mm Artificial Turf Pitch – Ideal for Box Cricket

A 15 mm thick artificial turf pitch is suitable for box cricket, and it's a popular choice due to its durability and safety in compact play areas.

Why It's Suitable for Box Cricket:

  • Good for Tennis Ball or Light Leather Ball: Provides realistic bounce and ball movement ideal for small formats.
  • Shock Absorption: The thickness ensures comfort and safety in fast-paced play.
  • Low Maintenance: Best for closed spaces where maintenance is tough.
  • Durability in High-Impact Zones: Handles quick footwork and ball strikes well.
  • Indoor/Outdoor Use: Perfect over concrete for both indoor and outdoor box cricket.

Standard Cricket Practice Turf – 25m x 2m

A 25-meter length and 2-meter width artificial turf roll is the ideal and standard size for a practice cricket pitch, especially in net areas or personal setups.

Why This Size Works Perfectly:

  • Matches pitch length: Covers the official 22-yard (20.12 m) pitch with extra space at both ends for bowler run-up and foot safety.
  • Convenient width: 2 meters gives enough room for batting and bowling while keeping costs and space efficient.
  • One-piece roll: Avoids joins or seams that could affect bounce or durability.

Ideal for:

  • Coaching academies
  • School grounds
  • Home/backyard setups
  • Turf installations for net practice or training centers

Benefits for Leather Ball Cricket

1. Consistent Bounce & Pace

When laid over a hard surface (like concrete), the 15 mm turf provides a reliable bounce—ideal for batsmen to practice shot timing and bowlers to focus on line and length.

2. Reduced Wear & Tear

Leather balls can damage natural pitches quickly. This turf protects both the ball and the base surface, extending usability.

3. Low Maintenance

Unlike natural turf that needs rolling, watering, and mowing, this artificial turf just needs occasional cleaning.

4. Year-Round Usability

Weather-resistant and quick-drying, it can be used in most conditions—even during monsoon when natural pitches are unusable.

5. Safer on Joints

The 15 mm thickness provides mild shock absorption, helpful for bowlers who practice repeatedly.

Conclusion

If you're looking for a long-term, low-maintenance practice solution, especially for leather ball use, this pitch is very economical and cost-effective over 5+ years.
